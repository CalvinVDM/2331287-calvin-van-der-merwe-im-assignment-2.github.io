Week 4 - What is a browser? How do they work?

According to Mozilla a web browser is something that that takes you anywhere on the internet. This means that it will take you to any available place on the internet such as websites and search engines. A web browser gets information from these parts on the internet and displays it on the device you are using. This information is sent using http (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) which is used to tell the device and browser how text, videos and other forms of media are transmitted on the internet. (Mozilla, n.d.)

Examples of web browsers include google chrome, internet explorer and opera. These browsers are used by devices to input information and search the internet for that information to display to the user. All browsers do not use and interpret information the same and so this means that on different browsers websites will look and function differently.

Browsers use software called rendering engines to translate the data they receive from the internet into images and text. Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) is the language that the data is written in, and the rendering engines reads this code to provide us with the things we experience on the internet.

The main job of browsers consists of DNS resolution, HTTP exchange, Rendering and repeat. DNs resolution is the process where one enters a URL, and the browser will know which server need to connect to. The browser talks to this server in order to get and IP address so that the browser can find what the URL wants to display.

HTTP exchange happens when our browser communicates with servers in order to get the information we are looking for. Is an exchange of a request from out browser and a reply from the server where we want the data.

Rendering is the process using the data we receive to render the text style images and visuals we see on whatever website we are looking for. Its is the combination of Html, CSS and JavaScript that creates and interactable website for use to see on our devices. (freeCodeCamp, 2018)

Week 6 – The uses of Metadata – what, why, how

Metadata is often referred to as data that describes other data, its known as structured reference data that helps to sort and find out about the information is describes. The word “Meta is a prefix that basically means “and underlying definition or description” which mean that metadata is information that tell us more about the data we are looking at. This can make it easier to find and use instances of data and to see where it was found and use. (Kranz, n.d.)

Why do we use metadata? We use metadata because it helps to improve the data we are using. The metadata makes up for the mistakes people make as people often lose or misplace data, so metadata is way to find the data we were using, and it makes sure we preserve and can re-use this data in the future. It also helps to show the credibility of the data we are using by showing when it was created or edited, and it also shown who created or edited it. (UNC, n.d.)

Metadata is created anytime a document is modified or even deleted this is used to prolong and find data wherever we need it, and it will always make sure that the data we are using comes from a source and we can identify where or who that source is. It is useful as it basically shows us the history of any data we use and can help us if we ever need to verify data or find this data to use. (Kranz, n.d.)

I am analysing Graham et al’s “Toward a study of information geographies”. I found the content of this reading to be quite extraordinary and interesting as it dealt with concepts that I have never touched upon or thought about. The reading talks about the development of information based on its location and access geographically and how these can be improved so that this information is provided to everyone and so that everyone has access to it.

Introduction: information geographies and (im)mutable augmentations

This chapter talks about the fact that information has always had a geography meaning that it originated, circulated as well as cultivated in certain areas. This tells us that because of the geography of information the way that information evolved was because of where it was used and so it developed based on its surroundings and area. This logic is then applied to the current era and talks about how the history relates to the development of information. It is then also explained how information was adapted to be more mobile and to physical so that it could be transported to different areas this was when information was written and condensed into physical copies such as books.

This talks about how when information is left out certain things can be forgot or changes due to the lack of acknowledgement. An example is given that says if a restaurant is omitted from a map that the restaurant can cease to be a restaurant due to lack of service and lack of information regarding its location and services. This also talks about how much information we have on places and things such as places that provides good and or services.

Pre-digital geographies of information

This chapter talks about how information was stored and transferred before technology such as computers and tablets came into play. This therefore tells us about how tools and written pieces of information were used to gather more information or to understand certain things and concepts, like a compass or direction or a ruler for measurement. This chapter goes on to explain the circulation of information in areas and how the fact that the information is physical would affect how it develops.

This means that due to the physical nature of the storage of information it was harder to transmit over a larger area such as a country, it would be harder for people of one side of the country to know of what happened on the other side of the country due to the circulation of newspapers not being a fast medium as it does not transfer information instantaneously.

Changing geographies after the information revolution?

This chapter talks about the fact that due to the revolution and development of data information has become a lot less localised and is now very easy to access almost everywhere in the world. Since information has now become digital it is easier to transfer information at high speeds to anyplace in the world if there is a device to interpret it. This means that if one has a device that can receive and communicate the data to the user of said device then one will have access to information of almost any kind that has been recorded as digital data.

This chapter therefore talks about the power of the internet as a medium of transferring and relating data to people across the world and so how if one has access to the internet, they have access to this information as a moments notice. This made is so that the geographical placing of a person would not prohibit them from having access to all kinds of information.

Geographies of access and enablement

This chapter talks about the fact that digital devices are not always affordable and owned by people and so now that distance is not the problem that prohibits the connection of people to information but rather it is now the fact that people either do not have the device to access the internet so they have the information or that the people are unable to afford the price for bandwidth so that their device is able to access the internet so that they have this information.

This chapter talks about the fact the most internet usage is concentrated in largely well-developed countries and that it’s because of affordability that many other countries and people are left out from using the information stored on the internet.

Geographies of participation

This chapter talks about the countries in the world that participate mostly in the orchestration of gathering and presenting information on the internet. This again looks at the geographical location of the amount of people and participates in the exchange and gathering of information on the internet and it is again seen and displayed that the countries that have a more well-developed economy will usually have more participation which means once again the geographical location and the circumstances of people will affect the access to information that those people have.

It again becomes an issue of access to the pools of information gathered by society.

Geographies of representation

This chapter is focused on the way that information is provided and perceived by people due to their geographical location. This issue deals with the fact that across the world different languages are spoken and read meaning that for all the information to be in one certain language would once again limit and prohibit the access of information to certain individuals and people. This means that depending on where one was raised their ability to understand the information provided by the internet or by books will differ. If someone is raised in a place where they learn to speak and write Spanish or another language it will become hard to understand and interpret the information on the internet that is provided in English.

Conclusions: towards a study of information geographies

The conclusion to this topic talks about the fact that information needs to unfix and fixed at the same time meaning that information should not be constrained to books or the internet or newspapers but rather provided by any means that is available and that information should be fixed depending on the geographical location of people. This means that the information should be available in as many formats as possible but should also be easy to understand regardless on one’s location and language.

The conclusion states that information is meant to be all inclusive to everyone regardless of circumstance and geography.

What is Society?

A society is an organised group of persons associated together for religious, benevolent, cultural, scientific, political, patriotic, or other purposes (dictionary, n.d.). this means that people in a society usually come from similar backgrounds and follow the same conventions and ideologies.

Due to the internet being apart of modern society we can see that regarding the modern era the internet is one of the most useful and influential tools in society. It allows for things like communication across vast distances and even helps to provide information on any topic to its users. Majority of people today live a lot of their lives on the internet using social media and using other services the internet provides. This means that society is now connected throughout the entire world and that a lot of people are now able to communicate their conventions and ideologies in a way that most people around the global will be able to see.

We call this global connectivity which allows everyone in the world to connect and talk to each other through the internet and the use of broadband which allows one to access the internet. There are even cases of identity theft and kidnaping that is enabled by the internet. (Daniolou, 2020)

What is design justice?

Design Justice is and an exploration of how design might be led by marginalized communities, dismantle structural inequality, and advance collective liberation and ecological survival (Costanza-Chock, 2020). This explores the relationship between design, power, and social justice. It’s a way to make sure that when making a design that the design is all inclusive and safe for anyone it doesn’t exclude any groups of people and does cause harm or exclusion to anyone.

The purpose is to make sure that everyone has fair treatment, acknowledgement, and inclusion in the design of something. This fits into the internet by making the internet accessible to everyone and making sure that everyone can use it as well. Meaning that there is no language barrier or anything else that inhibits a person from using the internet to their advantage.
